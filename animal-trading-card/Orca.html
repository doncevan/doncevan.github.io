Orca

Orcas, also known as killer whales, are actually the largest members of the dolphin family. Despite their name, they are not whales at all.

  • Habitat: Most often in coastal waters
  • Lifespan: Up to 100 years
  • Size: Leight: 5-8m
  • Weight: 1,4-5,4 tons
  • Diet: Fish, penguins and marine mammals
  • Top Speed: 56 km/h (35 mph)

Orcas are the largest members of the dolphin family, known for their black and white coloring. They inhabit oceans worldwide, from polar to tropical regions. These apex predators eat fish, seals, and whales. Living in matriarchal pods, they communicate with various vocalizations and showcase their intelligence through cooperative hunting.
